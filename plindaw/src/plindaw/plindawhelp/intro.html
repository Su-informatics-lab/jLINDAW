pLINDAW: A Fuzzy Query Based Data Warehouse System for Real-time Pan-LINCS Data Integration and Visualization

The Library of Integrated Network-Based Cellular Signatures (LINCS) program founded by the National Institute of Health has been generating large and diverse datasets to systematically characterize the effects of perturbations on cellular disease model system. Potentially an invaluable resource to a wide range of research fields, these datasets are highly heterogeneous in terms of disparate focuses and evoluting standards and formats. The Integrative and Translational Network-based Cellular Signature Analyzer (itNETZ) platform, developed by our Wake Forest LINCS Computation Center, systematically addresses these Big Data challenges and bridges such abundant LINCS resources and the needs of general LINCS users.

The pLINDAW system incorporates LINCS data and itNETZ discoveries (Figure 1 A), allows flexible data integration by fuzzy querying using a number of similarity metrics, and supports on-the-fly data modeling and visualization. Currently a java-based application, jLINDAW (Figure 1 B), is available for general users, and a direct access to the data warehouse for advanced users. The pLINDAW system is distinguished by the signature-based data integration and similarity-based fuzzy query.
